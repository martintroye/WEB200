Welcome to my bio. Hopefully this page will give you a little insight into who I am and what I have been doing since High School.

Education

In 2015 I graduated from the University of Nebraska Lincoln with a bachelor’s degree in psychology with a minor in French. During college I volunteered teaching English to adults through the Lincoln Literacy Program was a member of the Alpha Xi Delta sorority and played clarinet in the marching band.

Experience

After college I applied and was accepted into the Peace Corps. I was stationed in Benin Africa from June of 2016 to August of 2018. In Benin I lived in a small village in the northern part of the country and taught the equivalent of middle school English. While in Benin I participated or organized several community projects such as: hand washing stations at the school, a mural of the globe painted on a school wall, English club, regional teacher training and sponsoring events to empower young women in the community. For more information on the Peace Corps or Benin here are a couple of helpful sites. • https://www.peacecorps.gov/about/history/ • https://www.peacecorps.gov/benin/ • https://www.britannica.com/place/Benin#ref516985 • https://www.bbc.com/news/world-africa-13037572 To see where I was stationed here is a link to a map showing the area, I lived in. • https://goo.gl/maps/iEL6rnawn5A2

Certifications

From my teaching experience in the Peace Corps I became TEFL certified. TEFL stands for Teach English as a Foreign Language. To find out more about this certification please visit https://www.tefl.org/us/. I continue to teach adults English part-time through Metropolitan Community College.

Hobbies

In my spare time I enjoy cooking, video games, reading and playing with our dog Millie. I also like to run and have completed two half marathons. Besides Benin I have been fortunate to have visited several other countries including Spain, France, Togo and Ghana.
